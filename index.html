Study Techniques & Methods

Different Techniques of Studying

Effective studying requires more than just reading material. Here are proven techniques:

  • Active Recall: Testing yourself without looking at the material
  • Spaced Repetition: Reviewing information at increasing intervals
  • Interleaving: Mixing different subjects or topics during study sessions
  • Elaboration: Explaining concepts in your own words
  • Dual Coding: Combining verbal and visual information
  • Concrete Examples: Using specific examples to understand abstract ideas

Each technique taps into different cognitive processes, making some more suitable for certain subjects or learning styles than others.

Study Methods: Strengths & Weaknesses

Pomodoro Technique

Strengths: Prevents burnout, maintains focus, manages time effectively

Weaknesses: Can disrupt deep work flow, not ideal for all tasks

Feynman Technique

Strengths: Uncovers knowledge gaps, enhances understanding, simplifies complex topics

Weaknesses: Time-consuming, not efficient for memorization tasks

SQ3R Method

Strengths: Improves comprehension, active reading, better retention

Weaknesses: Slow process, not suitable for all reading materials

Leitner System

Strengths: Efficient flashcards, spaced repetition, tracks progress

Weaknesses: Requires setup time, less effective for conceptual subjects

Blurting Method

Strengths: Quick knowledge assessment, identifies weak areas, active recall

Weaknesses: Can be demotivating if knowledge gaps are large, requires prior study
